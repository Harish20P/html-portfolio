The Best Movies According To Me

My top 3 movies of all time

Avengers EndGame

'Avengers: Endgame' expertly concludes the Marvel Cinematic Universe's 21-film saga, seamlessly blending action, emotion, comedy, and drama. Directors Anthony and Joe Russo navigate high expectations with finesse, while writers Christopher Markus and Stephen McFeely deliver an engaging screenplay for both fans and newcomers.
Despite uneven screen time, each character plays a significant role, and unexpected twists keep the narrative compelling. The film's emotional depth, brought to life by stellar performances, adds a layer of complexity, even to the antagonist, Thanos, portrayed by Josh Brolin.
Balancing intense action with narrative relevance, 'Endgame' surprises fans and maintains visual appeal through CGI work. While non-fans might find the explanatory tone a speed bump, enthusiasts are treated to constant excitement and cheers.
At over three hours, 'Avengers: Endgame' fulfills its promises, leaving a lasting impact on fans and serving as a fitting tribute to the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

Harry Potter

The "Harry Potter" film series, comprised of eight movies, is widely praised for faithfully adapting J.K. Rowling's beloved book series. With strong performances from the cast, impressive visual effects, and a seamless transition from lighter tones to darker themes, the films successfully capture the magic and essence of the wizarding world. The series, led by Daniel Radcliffe, Emma Watson, and Rupert Grint, is celebrated for its character development and intricate plot, making it a cinematic achievement that appeals to both fans of the books and new audiences.

Titanic

"Titanic," directed by James Cameron, is a timeless romantic drama set against the backdrop of the ill-fated maiden voyage of the RMS Titanic. Released in 1997, the film stars Leonardo DiCaprio and Kate Winslet. It received widespread acclaim for its epic storytelling, stunning visuals, and compelling performances. The film's portrayal of the tragic sinking of the Titanic, combined with the poignant love story between Jack and Rose, has made it a classic in cinematic history. Despite its lengthy runtime, "Titanic" remains an enduring and emotionally resonant film.
